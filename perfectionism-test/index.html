The key distinction

The question is not whether you have high standards but whether they can be satisfied. Anxiety perfectionism has a movable goalpost. Genuine standards can actually be met and produce real satisfaction.

The highest leverage change

Practising deliberately finishing work that is good rather than perfect, and observing what actually happens, is the most direct way to start updating the beliefs that maintain anxiety-driven perfectionism.

The cost at this level

At a high level, anxiety perfectionism produces procrastination through fear of starting, inability to complete because nothing is ever quite ready, exhaustion from the sustained effort, and the loss of genuine satisfaction from any achievement.

Self-compassion is not lowering standards

Research consistently shows self-compassion is associated with better performance than self-criticism. The intuition that harsh self-criticism produces quality is wrong. It produces anxiety that impairs quality.

What changes this

CBT for perfectionism targets the beliefs that maintain it: the catastrophising about imperfection and the equation of performance with worth. Behavioural experiments, deliberately submitting imperfect work and observing actual consequences, are the most directly effective intervention.
