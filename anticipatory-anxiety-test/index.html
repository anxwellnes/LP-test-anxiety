The core pattern
Anticipatory anxiety works by treating future events as immediately threatening even though they have not happened and may not unfold as feared. The same mechanisms that maintain general anxiety maintain anticipatory anxiety: catastrophising and intolerance of uncertainty.
The most useful recognition
The fact that the anticipatory anxiety is usually worse than the event itself is important evidence. It suggests the predictions the anxiety is making are systematically inaccurate. Each instance where the dread exceeded the reality is evidence against the anxiety predictions.

Why this matters
High anticipatory anxiety has a compounding effect on quality of life because the suffering occurs twice: once in the dread before the event and once if the event is difficult. For events that go reasonably well, the dread was the entire cost.
What maintains it
Avoidance is the primary maintaining mechanism. Every avoided event confirms the prediction that the event was too threatening to face. Exposure, gradually and systematically facing anticipated events, is the most directly effective intervention.
