    How to identify which type of anxiety you have

    Different anxiety types are distinguished primarily by their focus and their maintaining mechanisms. Generalised anxiety disorder involves pervasive worry across multiple domains with intolerance of uncertainty as the core feature. Social anxiety disorder centres on fear of negative evaluation in social and performance situations. Health anxiety involves preoccupation with physical symptoms and illness fears. Panic disorder involves acute episodes with significant fear of the episodes themselves.

    These patterns often overlap and frequently co-occur. The most important thing is not the diagnostic label but understanding which specific maintaining mechanisms are most prominent in your experience, because this determines which interventions will be most effective. For a broader severity assessment, the anxiety level test covers all dimensions.

    Key distinguishing features

    • GAD: worry across many topics, intolerance of uncertainty, persistent background anxiety
    • Social anxiety: fear of evaluation, avoidance of social situations, self-focused attention
    • Health anxiety: preoccupation with illness, body checking, reassurance seeking that provides only temporary relief
    • Panic disorder: acute episodes, fear of the episodes themselves, avoidance of associated situations

    Frequently asked questions

    What are the different types of anxiety disorders?

    The main types are generalised anxiety disorder, social anxiety disorder, health anxiety, panic disorder, OCD and specific phobias. Each has distinct features but shares the core mechanisms of anxious thinking and avoidance.

    Can you have more than one type of anxiety?

    Yes. Anxiety disorders frequently co-occur. Many people have elements of more than one type simultaneously. Treatment can address multiple patterns.

    Does the type of anxiety affect what treatment works?

    Yes. While CBT is effective across types, the specific techniques vary significantly by pattern. Getting the right approach for your specific type improves outcomes.

    How is this different from the anxiety level test?

    The anxiety level test measures overall severity. This quiz identifies which specific anxiety type is most prominent in your experience to direct you toward the most relevant resources and treatment approaches.
