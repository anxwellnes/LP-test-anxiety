Why this matters

Emotional exhaustion from anxiety creates a feedback loop: exhaustion reduces the cognitive resources available for managing anxiety, which increases the anxiety, which produces more exhaustion. Addressing this requires working on both simultaneously.

The most accessible intervention

Sleep quality improvement, regular exercise and reducing the anxiety-driven vigilance that consumes resource between episodes are the most direct routes to restoring reserves.

What this level means

At this level of depletion, the capacity to use the cognitive and behavioural skills that manage anxiety is itself compromised. The depleted nervous system is more reactive, less able to tolerate uncertainty, and less resilient to additional demands.

What is most important now

Genuine rest and genuine reduction of demands are the priority at this level, alongside professional support. Addressing the depletion is a prerequisite for more effective anxiety management.

Anxiety or burnout?

At this level, the pattern may have moved from anxiety-driven depletion into burnout territory. The anxiety vs burnout quiz can help you understand which you are primarily dealing with.
