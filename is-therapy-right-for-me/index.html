Why "right now" matters as much as "do I need it"

Most people who would benefit from therapy already know they have anxiety. The question that actually stops them is not whether therapy would help, but whether this is the right moment to start.

This assessment evaluates both: the severity of what you are experiencing and the specific factors that make now more or less the right time.

What makes this different from a general anxiety severity test

Anxiety severity tests measure symptoms. This assessment measures readiness — which combines how much anxiety is costing you, how far self-management has taken you, what is happening in your life right now, and what barriers are realistic versus perceived.

The result is a direct recommendation rather than a severity score.

What if the result says therapy is not right for me now?

That is a genuine result, not a consolation. Some people are managing effectively with their current resources, or are at a stage where building self-understanding first makes more sense than starting therapy. The result is honest and explains what to look for that would change the picture.

If the result does recommend therapy, the reasoning will be specific to your answers — not a generic recommendation that applies to everyone above a certain score.
