The key distinction

Overthinking feels productive because it resembles problem-solving. The difference is that productive thinking reaches conclusions and moves toward action. Overthinking cycles without resolution and without producing the clarity it seems to promise.

The highest leverage technique

Scheduled worry time, containing worry to a specific 15-20 minute window rather than allowing it at any time, is one of the most evidence-based techniques for reducing overthinking without suppressing thoughts directly.

What is maintaining it

At this level, overthinking is typically maintained by an underlying anxiety pattern rather than being a standalone habit. The thoughts are anxiety-driven rather than analytically useful, which is why they cycle without resolution.

What helps most

CBT and ACT both have strong evidence for the thought patterns that maintain significant overthinking. Techniques include thought defusion, scheduled worry, the usefulness question rather than the truth question, and building the capacity to tolerate uncertainty without compulsive mental resolution.
