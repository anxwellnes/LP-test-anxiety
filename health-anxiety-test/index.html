    What is health anxiety and how does it work?

    Health anxiety is a pattern of persistent, excessive worry about having or developing a serious illness. People with health anxiety often notice normal bodily sensations, a heartbeat, a tight muscle, a fleeting headache, and interpret them as symptoms of something dangerous. The more they focus on these sensations, the more intense they feel, which appears to confirm the fear.

    Unlike general anxiety, which tends to jump between many worries, health anxiety tends to fixate on the body. If you want to understand your overall anxiety levels, the free anxiety level test covers a broader range of anxiety patterns including worry, panic and overthinking.

    Common signs of health anxiety

    • Repeatedly checking your body for lumps, changes or symptoms
    • Googling symptoms and spending hours reading about diseases
    • Seeking reassurance from doctors, family or the internet only to feel better briefly before the worry returns
    • Avoiding medical appointments out of fear of what they might find
    • Interpreting normal physical sensations as evidence of serious illness
    • Feeling temporarily relieved after tests come back normal, then worrying again soon after
    • Noticing that your worry about health significantly affects your daily life

    What your health anxiety test score may mean

    Low health anxiety

    Some attention to your health is normal and healthy. At this level, health concerns do not appear to be significantly disrupting your life or thinking.

    Moderate health anxiety

    Health anxiety may be taking a noticeable toll on your peace of mind, your time and your ability to trust your body. The worry is real, and it deserves attention.

    High health anxiety

    Health anxiety may be significantly affecting your quality of life, your relationships and your ability to feel safe in your own body. This level responds very well to the right therapeutic support and does not have to be permanent.

    Frequently asked questions

    What is health anxiety?

    Health anxiety is a persistent pattern of excessive worry about having or developing a serious illness, often despite normal test results and medical reassurance. It involves body checking, symptom searching and reassurance seeking that provides only temporary relief.

    Is health anxiety the same as hypochondria?

    Yes. Health anxiety is the modern clinical term for what was previously called hypochondria. The underlying experience is the same, persistent illness worry that does not respond to reassurance.

    Can health anxiety cause real physical symptoms?

    Yes. Health anxiety can produce genuine physical sensations including palpitations, chest tightness, dizziness and nausea. These are caused by anxiety, not illness, but they feel completely real and often get misinterpreted as confirmation of the feared condition.

    Does health anxiety get better with therapy?

    Yes. Health anxiety is one of the most successfully treated anxiety conditions. Therapy helps you understand why reassurance seeking maintains the worry, and how to respond to symptoms and fears in ways that reduce anxiety rather than reinforce it.

    How is this different from the general anxiety level test?

    The anxiety level test measures overall anxiety severity. This test focuses specifically on health-related worry patterns, body checking, reassurance seeking and illness fear. If panic attacks are part of your experience, the Panic Attack SOS Card also provides a personalised crisis plan.
