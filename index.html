      How this anxiety test works

      This test covers 21 patterns linked to anxiety — worry, panic sensations, overthinking, avoidance, sleep disruption, concentration problems, and physical tension. Each answer is scored from Never (0) to Almost always (4). Your total maps to a percentage and one of three levels: low, moderate, or high.

      The goal is not to label you — it is to give you a clearer snapshot of how anxiety may be affecting your life right now, so you can decide what kind of support would actually help.

      What your score may mean

      Low anxiety

      Anxiety appears occasionally but is not strongly disrupting daily life. Focus is on prevention — keeping the patterns that help you stay steady.

      Moderate anxiety

      Anxiety is taking a noticeable toll on energy, focus, and calm. Many people still function at this level, but everything feels like more effort than it should.

      High anxiety

      Anxiety may be affecting multiple areas — thoughts, physical sensations, sleep, and concentration. This does not mean something is wrong with you; it often means the nervous system has been under too much strain for too long.

      Common anxiety symptoms

      • Constant worry or overthinking
      • Difficulty relaxing even when nothing serious is happening
      • Racing heart, chest tightness, shakiness, or dizziness
      • Panic feelings or sudden waves of fear
      • Trouble falling asleep because the mind will not slow down
      • Replaying conversations or situations for hours afterward
      • Expecting the worst even without clear evidence
      • Avoidance of situations that feel triggering or overwhelming

      Frequently asked questions

      What is an anxiety test?

      A self-assessment that asks about worry, tension, panic, overthinking, sleep, and daily functioning — helping you understand whether anxiety may be affecting you in a small, moderate, or stronger way.

      Is this free anxiety test a diagnosis?

      No. This test is for informational self-assessment only. It does not diagnose any mental health condition. If symptoms feel severe or persistent, speaking with a qualified professional is recommended.

      How long does it take?

      21 questions — usually around 3 minutes to complete.

      What do the results mean?

      Your result gives you a percentage score and a level label — designed to help you better understand the current impact anxiety may be having on your life.

      What should I do if I score moderate or high?

      A moderate or high result may suggest that anxiety deserves more intentional attention. Many people benefit from structured self-help, guided support, or speaking with a qualified professional.
