What is an anxiety level test?

An anxiety level test is a self-assessment that helps you understand how strongly anxiety may be affecting your thoughts, body, sleep, focus and daily life right now. Unlike a clinical diagnosis, a free anxiety test gives you an immediate snapshot so you can make better decisions about what kind of support or action would actually help.

This anxiety test online uses 21 questions and takes about 3 minutes. Your result is shown as a percentage and grouped into a low, moderate or high anxiety level, each with a clear explanation of what it means for you.

What is a Panic Attack SOS Card?

A Panic Attack SOS Card is a personalized step-by-step plan you can look at during a panic attack when your mind goes blank and you cannot remember what to do. Unlike generic advice, this card is built around your specific symptoms, your first thoughts during panic, and where you feel it most in your body.

The card is generated based on 8 questions and saved as an image you can keep on your phone. When panic hits, you open it and follow the steps, no thinking required.

Why these tools are different from generic anxiety advice

Most anxiety resources online give the same advice to everyone: breathe deeply, try mindfulness, talk to someone. That advice is not wrong but it is not personalized, and when you are in the middle of a panic attack or trying to understand a score, generic advice does not help.

These tools are built around one idea: your anxiety is not the same as everyone else's anxiety. The anxiety level test gives you a result based on your specific answers, not a one-size-fits-all score. The panic attack SOS card gives you steps based on your exact symptoms, your typical first thought, where you feel panic in your body, and what has helped you before.

Both tools are completely free, require no account, and give you something useful in under 3 minutes.
