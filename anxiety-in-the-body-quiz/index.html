Why these symptoms occur

Every physical symptom of anxiety is produced by sympathetic nervous system activation. Muscle tension prepares the body to fight or flee. Digestive changes redirect blood flow. Cardiovascular changes prepare for action. The body is responding to a perceived threat exactly as designed.

What helps physically

Diaphragmatic breathing directly counteracts the sympathetic activation. Regular exercise reduces baseline sympathetic tone. Progressive muscle relaxation addresses the tension. The anxiety body scan tool on this site is designed specifically for understanding and working with somatic anxiety.

The mind-body relationship

At this level of somatic anxiety, the physical symptoms are themselves an anxiety-generating trigger. The racing heart is interpreted as threat, which increases anxiety, which increases the heart rate. Breaking into this loop physiologically, through breathing and movement, is as important as the cognitive component.

Medical assessment first

If you have not had a medical assessment of your physical symptoms, it is always appropriate to rule out physical causes first. Once physical causes are excluded, addressing the anxiety that is producing the symptoms is the most effective route.

What helps most at this level

Body-based approaches, progressive muscle relaxation, diaphragmatic breathing, regular exercise and the techniques in the calm anxiety fast guide, are particularly important alongside cognitive work. The body scan tool can help you develop awareness of the physical anxiety pattern.
