Why these symptoms occur
Every physical symptom of anxiety is produced by the sympathetic nervous system activation. Muscle tension prepares the body to fight or flee. Digestive changes redirect blood flow. Cardiovascular changes prepare for action. The body is responding to a perceived threat exactly as it is designed to.
What helps physically
Diaphragmatic breathing directly counteracts the sympathetic activation. Regular exercise reduces baseline sympathetic tone. Progressive muscle relaxation addresses the tension. The anxiety body scan on this site is designed specifically for understanding and working with somatic anxiety.

The mind-body relationship
At this level of somatic anxiety, the physical symptoms are themselves an anxiety-generating trigger. The racing heart is interpreted as threat, which increases the anxiety, which increases the heart rate. Breaking into this loop physiologically, through breathing regulation and movement, is as important as the cognitive component.
Medical assessment first
If you have not had a medical assessment of your physical symptoms, it is always appropriate to rule out physical causes first. Once physical causes are excluded, addressing the anxiety that is producing the symptoms is the most effective route.
Body-based approaches
For strong somatic anxiety, approaches that work directly with the body, progressive muscle relaxation, diaphragmatic breathing, physical exercise and the techniques in the calm anxiety fast guide, are particularly important alongside the cognitive work.
