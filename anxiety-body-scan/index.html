Why anxiety shows up in your body

Anxiety is not just a mental experience. It is a full-body physiological response. When the brain perceives a threat, real or imagined, it activates the stress response, releasing adrenaline and cortisol that prepare the body for danger. Different people feel this activation in different parts of the body, and the pattern of where you feel it can tell you something meaningful about the type and intensity of your anxiety.

Understanding where anxiety lives in your body is the first step to developing more precise strategies for managing it. For a full assessment of how severely anxiety may be affecting your life overall, the free anxiety level test gives you a complete severity score.

The most common physical anxiety zones

  • Chest and heart — racing or pounding heart, tightness or pressure in the chest, difficulty taking a full breath
  • Stomach and abdomen — nausea, knots, butterflies, cramping or digestive disruption
  • Head — tension headaches, brain fog, dizziness or a feeling of pressure behind the eyes
  • Throat — tightness, difficulty swallowing or a lump-like sensation
  • Shoulders and neck — chronic tension, aching or stiffness that does not resolve with rest
  • Arms and hands — trembling, tingling, numbness or restless hands
