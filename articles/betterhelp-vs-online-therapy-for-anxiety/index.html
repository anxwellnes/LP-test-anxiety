BetterHelp vs Online-Therapy.com for Anxiety: Which Should You Choose?

Updated March 2026 · 12 min read · Comparison

BetterHelp is the most recognised name in online therapy. Online-Therapy.com is smaller, more specialised, and built almost entirely around cognitive behavioural therapy. If you have anxiety and you are trying to decide between the two, the right answer depends on what kind of support you actually need.

This comparison covers the structure of each platform, what they include, how their therapist pools differ, cost, and which type of person each one suits best.

How they work: a structural difference that matters

BetterHelp matches you with a licensed therapist and gives you access to messaging, live chat, phone sessions, and video sessions. The therapy itself is unstructured, meaning the therapist decides the approach. Some use CBT, some use solution-focused therapy, some use psychodynamic approaches. You may or may not be assigned structured homework.

Online-Therapy.com works differently. Every subscriber gets access to a structured eight-section CBT program that runs in parallel with therapist access. You complete worksheets, the therapist reviews them and comments, and your live sessions (if included in your plan) are informed by the work you have already done. The therapeutic approach is CBT across the board.

For anxiety specifically, this structural difference is significant. CBT is the most evidence-supported treatment for anxiety disorders. Knowing that CBT is the framework before you start is not a minor detail.

Therapist quality: what the research says

Both platforms require licensed therapists. BetterHelp has a much larger pool, which increases variety but also increases inconsistency. Because BetterHelp therapists use different modalities, your experience depends heavily on who you are matched with. Some users report excellent CBT-trained therapists. Others describe sessions that feel more like supportive conversations without structure.

Online-Therapy.com's pool is smaller but more consistent in its approach. Because every therapist works within a CBT framework and has access to your worksheet progress, the floor of quality tends to be higher for anxiety-specific work. You are less likely to end up with someone who uses an approach with weaker evidence for anxiety.

Cost comparison

BetterHelp charges per week, with prices varying based on location, demand, and time of year. The total monthly cost tends to be higher than Online-Therapy.com's mid-tier plans. Online-Therapy.com is transparent with fixed pricing, and new users through MyAnxietyTest qualify for a 20% discount, which brings the cost down further.

For the same approximate spend, you get more sessions per month on Online-Therapy.com, plus the structured CBT program that runs at no additional cost.

Who should choose BetterHelp?

Choose BetterHelp if...

You want a very large therapist pool, prefer phone sessions, need a non-CBT approach (EMDR, somatic, relational), or have already tried CBT and want something different.

Choose Online-Therapy.com if...

You have anxiety specifically, want structured CBT with real homework, value a therapist who reviews your progress between sessions, or want the best evidence-based approach at a lower cost.

The anxiety-specific verdict

For anxiety, Online-Therapy.com wins on structure and evidence. The combination of a structured CBT program and a therapist who can personalise it to your patterns is genuinely more than what most people get from messaging-based platforms.

BetterHelp is a solid general therapy platform. If you need anxiety-specific, structured, evidence-based support rather than general mental health conversations, Online-Therapy.com is the more targeted choice.

A note on what neither platform can replace

Both BetterHelp and Online-Therapy.com are appropriate for mild to moderate anxiety. Neither is a substitute for in-person care when anxiety is severe, when there is a risk to safety, or when the anxiety is deeply entangled with trauma that requires specialist treatment. If your anxiety significantly impairs your ability to function, a GP or psychiatrist referral should come first.

For the large majority of people with anxiety who are managing day-to-day but want structured, professional support without waiting lists, either platform offers real help. For that group, Online-Therapy.com's CBT structure gives it a clear edge for anxiety specifically.
