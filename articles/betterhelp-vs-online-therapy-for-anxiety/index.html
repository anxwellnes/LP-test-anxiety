BetterHelp vs Online-Therapy.com for Anxiety: Which Should You Choose?

📖 10 min read 🧠 MyAnxietyTest

BetterHelp is the most recognised name in online therapy. Online-Therapy.com is smaller, more specialised, and built almost entirely around cognitive behavioural therapy. If you have anxiety and are deciding between the two, the right answer depends on what kind of support you actually need.

This comparison covers the structure of each platform, what they include, how their therapist pools differ, cost, and which type of person each one suits best. If you are not sure whether you need professional support at all, the Do I Need Therapy quiz is worth doing first.

How they work: a structural difference that matters

BetterHelp matches you with a licensed therapist and gives you access to messaging, live chat, phone sessions, and video sessions. The therapy itself is unstructured, meaning the therapist decides the approach. Some use CBT, some use solution-focused therapy, some use psychodynamic approaches.

Online-Therapy.com works differently. Every subscriber gets access to a structured eight-section CBT program that runs in parallel with therapist access. You complete worksheets, the therapist reviews them and comments, and your live sessions are informed by the work you have already done. The therapeutic approach is CBT across the board.

For anxiety specifically, this structural difference is significant. CBT is the most evidence-supported treatment for anxiety disorders. Knowing that CBT is the framework before you start is not a minor detail.

Therapist quality: what the research says

Both platforms require licensed therapists. BetterHelp has a much larger pool, which increases variety but also inconsistency. Because BetterHelp therapists use different modalities, your experience depends heavily on who you are matched with. Some users report excellent CBT-trained therapists. Others describe sessions that feel like supportive conversations without structure.

Online-Therapy.com's pool is smaller but more consistent in approach. Because every therapist works within a CBT framework and has access to your worksheet progress, the floor of quality tends to be higher for anxiety-specific work.

Who should choose BetterHelp?

BetterHelp is the better choice if you want a very large therapist pool, prefer phone sessions, need a non-CBT approach such as EMDR or somatic therapy, or have already tried CBT and want something different. It is a solid general-purpose therapy platform.

Who should choose Online-Therapy.com?

Online-Therapy.com is the better choice if you have anxiety specifically, want structured CBT with real between-session homework, value a therapist who reviews your progress, or want the best evidence-based approach at a lower cost. For anxiety, it is the more targeted choice.
