There are dozens of online therapy platforms now, and most of them do roughly the same thing: match you with a therapist and let you exchange messages. Online-Therapy.com takes a different approach, one that happens to be particularly well-suited for anxiety.

This review covers what the platform actually includes, how the CBT component works, what kind of anxiety it helps most, where it falls short, and whether the cost is justified compared to alternatives.

What is Online-Therapy.com?

Online-Therapy.com is a subscription-based mental health platform that combines licensed therapist access with a structured cognitive behavioural therapy (CBT) program. It launched in 2009 and has since focused almost exclusively on CBT as its therapeutic framework, which makes it distinct in a market where many platforms offer therapists from any background with no particular methodology.

The platform is available in most countries and allows users to communicate with therapists via messaging, live video, or live chat sessions depending on the plan chosen. The CBT program runs in parallel and consists of worksheets, videos, journals, and activity trackers that a therapist can review and comment on between sessions.

The CBT program: what it actually looks like

This is where Online-Therapy.com earns its reputation. The CBT program is divided into eight sections covering core CBT concepts: identifying automatic thoughts, challenging cognitive distortions, behavioural activation, relaxation techniques, and relapse prevention. Each section includes short instructional videos, a written worksheet and a journal prompt.

The worksheets are not filler. They walk you through thought records, exposure hierarchies, and behavioural experiments in a structured format that mirrors what a therapist would assign in a traditional CBT session. The platform tracks your progress, and your assigned therapist can see which worksheets you have completed and add personalised comments directly.

💡 Why CBT matters for anxiety specifically: CBT has more research evidence behind it for anxiety disorders than any other psychological treatment. Unlike supportive therapy or general counselling, CBT directly targets the thought patterns and avoidance behaviours that maintain anxiety. A platform built around CBT is not just a preference, it is a clinically relevant choice for anxiety.

Which types of anxiety does it help most?

Based on the structure of the program and the evidence base for CBT, Online-Therapy.com is particularly well-suited for generalised anxiety disorder, social anxiety, health anxiety, panic disorder, and phobias. The exposure-focused sections are especially valuable for panic and specific phobias, where the evidence for graded exposure as a treatment is very strong.

It is less suited for complex trauma, severe OCD requiring specialised ERP protocols, or anxiety that is significantly entangled with personality disorder patterns. In those cases, a more specialised in-person service is likely a better fit.

Therapist quality and matching

All therapists on the platform are licensed in their respective countries, with most holding credentials equivalent to a licensed clinical social worker, licensed professional counsellor, or psychologist. The matching process asks about your primary concerns, preferred therapist gender, and whether you have preferences around religious or cultural background.

The matching is faster than most platforms, typically within 24 hours of signing up. You can request a different therapist at any time without giving a reason, which reduces the friction of finding someone who is a good fit.

The main limitation is that therapists on the platform are trained in CBT as a primary modality. If you are looking for someone with deep expertise in EMDR, somatic therapy, or psychodynamic approaches, this is not the right platform. For straightforward anxiety with a preference for structured, evidence-based work, the therapist pool is strong.

How does the cost compare?

Traditional in-person CBT from a private therapist typically costs between £70 and £180 per session in the UK, or $100 to $250 in the United States. At that rate, four sessions per month could cost between $400 and $1,000, without any structured homework program between sessions.

Online-Therapy.com's Premium plan, which includes four live sessions per month, is a fraction of that cost. For people who cannot afford private therapy, are on a waiting list, or live in an area without accessible mental health services, the cost difference is significant.

Who is it not right for?

Online-Therapy.com is not suitable if you are in crisis, experiencing suicidal ideation, or dealing with a psychiatric condition that requires medication review or intensive support. It is also not a substitute for in-person care when trauma is severe, when you are struggling to function day-to-day, or when previous attempts at self-directed therapy have not worked. In those situations, talking to a GP or psychiatrist first is the better path.

It is also not ideal if you strongly prefer completely open-ended, exploratory therapy without a structured program. Some people find the worksheet format motivating, others find it too clinical. That is an honest difference in fit, not a flaw in the platform.

Is it worth it for anxiety?

For most people with moderate anxiety who want structured, evidence-based support without the cost or waiting times of in-person therapy, yes. The CBT program alone is more thorough than most apps or self-help books, and the addition of a therapist who can personalise the work to your specific patterns gives it a clear advantage over doing CBT on your own.

The question to ask yourself is not whether online therapy is as good as the best possible in-person therapist. It usually is not. The real question is whether structured online CBT with a licensed therapist is better than what you are currently doing. For most people with untreated or poorly managed anxiety, the answer is clearly yes.
