How Much Does Therapy for Anxiety Cost? What to Know Before You Start

📖 7 min read 🧠 MyAnxietyTest

Cost is one of the most frequently cited barriers to starting therapy. Understanding what therapy actually costs, including the options that make it significantly more accessible, is one of the most practical things you can do before deciding whether to pursue it.

In-person private therapy

The cost of private in-person therapy varies significantly by location, therapist experience and approach. In the United States, sessions typically range from $100 to $250 per hour, with higher rates in major cities and from more experienced practitioners. In the UK, private therapy typically ranges from £60 to £150 per session. In Australia, between AUD $120 and $250.

At weekly sessions, the annual cost of private therapy can be substantial. This is the figure that most people think of when they think about therapy cost, and it is the one that most often leads people to conclude that therapy is unaffordable.

Online therapy: significantly more accessible

Online therapy platforms have substantially reduced the cost of therapy while maintaining clinical quality. Most major online therapy platforms offer subscription models that bring the effective cost per session down significantly compared to private in-person therapy.

These platforms employ qualified therapists and use evidence-based approaches. The cost reduction comes from the platform model, not from a reduction in therapist qualification or treatment quality. For many people, online therapy represents the most financially accessible route to qualified professional support.

Free and reduced-cost options

  • NHS therapy in the UK through IAPT, Improving Access to Psychological Therapies, offers free CBT for anxiety. Waiting times can be significant but the service is widely available
  • Employee Assistance Programmes, EAPs, offered by many employers provide a number of free therapy sessions per year as a workplace benefit
  • University training clinics offer reduced-cost therapy with supervised trainee therapists whose work is of good quality and properly overseen
  • Sliding scale fees: many private therapists offer reduced fees based on income. It is always worth asking
  • Community mental health centres often offer reduced-cost or free services based on income

The cost of not getting help

When evaluating the cost of therapy, it is worth considering it alongside the cost of not getting help. Anxiety that continues to escalate affects work performance, relationship quality, physical health and quality of life in ways that have their own financial and personal costs. Therapy that addresses the pattern early is both more effective and less expensive in total than therapy pursued after years of escalation.

The question is not just what therapy costs but what the ongoing cost of unaddressed anxiety is.

How to get started

If cost is a barrier, online therapy platforms offer the most accessible combination of qualified therapists and reasonable pricing. Many offer a free initial consultation or a significantly discounted first session.
