What you actually get day to day
The structural difference that matters most once you have actually signed up

This is the distinction that tends to matter far more in practice than it appears to on a pricing page. BetterHelp's model centres on the relationship with your matched therapist: you talk, by video, phone, or text, and between sessions you can message them, but there is no built in curriculum guiding what happens in the time between conversations. For many people that open structure works fine. For others, particularly with anxiety specifically, the lack of a concrete framework between sessions means the actual behaviour change work, the part that produces lasting results, depends entirely on what gets covered live and remembered afterward.

Online-Therapy.com takes a different approach entirely. Every paid plan includes the same eight section CBT program, regardless of tier, covering the core skills and exercises that structured cognitive behavioral therapy is built around. Your therapist responds to your specific worksheets and provides written feedback, which means the work you do alone during the week feeds directly into what your therapist actually addresses, rather than starting each session from a blank page. The added tools, daily journal, guided yoga and meditation videos, progress tracking tests, give the self directed time between sessions an actual shape, instead of leaving it to whatever you happen to remember from the last conversation.

BetterHelp's pricing scales with demand
Several independent reviews note that BetterHelp's weekly pricing fluctuates based on location and current therapist demand, meaning the price you are quoted can differ meaningfully from someone else's, and is not always the lower figure advertised.
