The anxiety connection

People pleasing is typically anxiety-driven rather than generosity-driven. The behaviour is maintained by the anxiety of disappointing or being disliked, not by genuine selflessness.

The cost over time

Moderate people pleasing produces resentment, depletes energy and erodes a sense of authentic self. The relationships it is trying to protect are often harmed by the inauthenticity it requires.

What is driving this

At this level, people pleasing is almost certainly anxiety-driven. The fear of disapproval, rejection or conflict is intense enough that it consistently overrides your own needs and preferences.

The resentment cycle

Strong people pleasing produces chronic resentment because you are consistently giving more than you want to give. This resentment builds and eventually damages the relationships the behaviour was meant to protect.

What helps most

CBT directly addresses the anxiety-driven beliefs that maintain people pleasing, particularly the catastrophising about the consequences of disappointing others. Exposure to saying no in small ways builds evidence that the feared outcomes do not materialise.
